Here’s an original short piece (30 lines), somewhere between a story and a poem:

---

The streetlights hummed like tired thoughts above the empty road
I walked home with my hands in my pockets and no destination
The wind kept trying to tell me something, but forgot the words
A plastic bag danced in circles like it was practicing escape

Somewhere behind me, a train sighed into the distance
I wondered if it was carrying people who knew exactly where they were going
That felt like a different species entirely

The corner shop blinked its neon sign twice before settling down
Inside, a man counted coins as if they might change shape
I bought nothing, just stood there pretending I might

Outside again, the night had rearranged itself
Pavements glistened like they had secrets under their skin
A cat crossed the road with courtroom confidence
Like it owned every silence it stepped through

I thought about calling someone
Not because I had anything to say
But because voices sometimes prove you still exist

My phone stayed dark in my palm
Like it was waiting for permission to matter

A bus passed, almost empty, carrying fluorescent loneliness
I watched it go like watching a thought disappear

Somewhere far away, someone laughed too loudly
And for a second, the world felt less heavy

I kept walking anyway
Not forward, not backward
Just through the space between moments
Where nothing asks you to become anything else

And that, for now, was enough
