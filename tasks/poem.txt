It started as a sentence and forgot where it was going.

There was no plan, no outline waiting patiently in the background—just a
beginning that arrived too quickly and a direction that never quite followed.
The words came anyway, one by one, as if they were trying to build something
out of nothing.

They hesitated at first. Soft, uncertain things. Words that didn’t quite belong
together but refused to leave each other alone. They gathered in loose lines,
drifting closer, testing meaning like it was something fragile that could break
if handled too roughly.

There was no rhythm to guide them. No structure to hold them in place. Just
space—wide and open—and the quiet pressure to say something that mattered.

Sometimes they almost did.

A phrase would land just right, carrying a weight it didn’t fully understand. A
line would stretch a little further than expected, brushing against something
real before slipping away again. And in those moments, it felt less like
writing and more like discovering something that had been waiting there all
along.

But it never stayed.

The meaning shifted too quickly, dissolving as soon as it formed. What felt
clear one second blurred into something unrecognizable the next. Still, the
words continued, drawn forward by something they couldn’t name.

Maybe it wasn’t about arriving anywhere.

Maybe it was enough to exist in the attempt—in the space between thoughts, in
the pauses where something almost true could be felt but not fully explained.

No rhythm, no rules,
just a heartbeat between lines,
trying to say something real
before the silence returned.

And when it did,
the silence didn’t feel empty.

It felt like an ending
that understood
it had also been a beginning.
